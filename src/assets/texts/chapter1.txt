<CT> What's in a name?

“There are only two hard things in Computer Science: cache invalidation and naming things.”

— Phil Karlton

“What's in a name? That which we call a rose, by any other word would smell as sweet.”

- Romeo and Juliet, William Shakespeare


Juliet could not be more right, the smell will be the same, but some names are better than others. A name is just a reference to a reality, a distinguishable pattern. We could call a rose "Peter," "Lily," or "X," but we are opening the door to equivocation.

Our Art, like taxidermy, is naming things. We come up with names all the time for the entities we conjure, but in a big and complex spell, keeping track of all those entities is a demonic task. Phil was right as well.

Naming our creations is an intimate act that reveals our inner state. A misnomer is symptomatic of flaws in our conceptual understanding of the conundrum at hand. The entity we are conceiving will inherit our misconceptions.

The creatures we manifested are a crystallization of our mental models. Our spells are linguistic machines animated by vibrating energy.

There are many ways to attain our grail, but our success depends on how well our conception fits reality.

An alchemical flask is a vessel that holds the alchemical work that you are doing. It is a container that holds your material work, which is the work of transmuting objects within reality. The flask is what you put your work into so that you can keep it contained and controlled while you work with it. It is like a crucible, only instead of heating material in it, it serves as a container that holds your work while you do your transmutations.
An alchemical flask is an alchemy device that is used for mixing substances together to create potions. It is used to mix liquids and powders together. The flask allows you to mix these substances by shaking it in a way that mixes them together without any parts of the flask coming into contact with those substances. This helps to avoid contamination in the flasks, so that you can mix different substances together without having to worry about contamination.
The alchemical flask is a very important part of the alchemy process. Without the alchemical flask, it would be much more difficult to mix substances together.
